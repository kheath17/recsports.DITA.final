Jobs for Students

There are plenty of opportunities for students to get involved in recreational sports. Positions are offered ranging from instructing classes to marketing and supervising. Explore the options to find your best fit!

Facility Supervisor
This is a supervisory position that requires effective communication and leadership skills. As a facility supervisor, you will assume a great deal of responsibility. The employees that you supervise will recognize you as part of the administrative staff within Recreational Sports. You will be entrusted to monitor and supervise the recreational facilities and employees in the absence of department management.
Your primary responsibility will be to ensure that the facilities are operating in a safe, efficient manner. Employees and patrons alike will look upon you as the person in charge. Please consider yourself as a role model to all of our facility employees. Hold yourself responsible for your own actions and the actions of the employees you supervise. Doing so is not only important for the success of the department, but can also develop into life long skills for you in your professional career.
To be a facility supervisor, you must meet the following qualifications:
  • Have a thorough knowledge of policies and procedures.
  • Know all information provided to you by management.
  • Have been trained in CPR/AED, First Aid and BBP.
  • Be familiar with the job description of other facility employee.
  • Provide extra commitment.

Fitness Assistant

Intramural Sports Official
Every year, the Intramural Sports program hires 200+ student officials for 7+ team sport activities. Officials' responsibilities include but are not limited to: officiating team sport activities, ensuring the safety of participants through use of proper officiating mechanics and rules, and assisting with site and equipment management. No qualifications or skills are required and no experience is necessary. You will be provided with the training needed to officiate intramural contests from highly trained and skilled staff.
Hiring Expectations: All intramural officials are required to complete all payroll requirements in a timely manner. Visit"Employment Packets" to view the necessary paperwork. Additionally, officials must attend each night of training for the sport they wish to officiate (typically 4-5 nights; employees are compensated for this time). Finally, all employees must complete a Title IX and Retaliation training prior to working.
Additionally:
  • Starting pay rate is $9.75/hour.
  • Officiating apparel and equipment provided.
  • We do not prohibit staff from participating in intramural sports.

Lifeguard
As a Recreational Sport lifeguard, your primary responsibility is for the safety of anyone using the aquatics centers. Responsibilities include supervising all swimmers through proper scanning, consistent rule enforcement, prevention of accidents and injuries as well as providing emergency intervention when needed. In addition, lifeguards will provide individual assistance to members as needed.
Applicants must demonstrate proficiency and the ability to execute the following tests:
  • Swim 300 yards non-stop only using front crawl and breaststroke.
  • Brick retrieval in deep water.
  • Tread water for 2 minutes without the use of hands.
  • Perform all American Red Cross Lifeguard, First Aid, CPR and AED skills.
